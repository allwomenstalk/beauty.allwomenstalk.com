# All You Need to Know about Botox for Women Who Want to Look Younger Instantly ...

Botox is most commonly used for aesthetic and cosmetic purposes. It's a minimally invasive treatment that is most often used to smooth lines and wrinkles on the face. According to the experts, [Botox](https://albanylaser.ca/) is a great choice for facial rejuvenation and for taking years off your face. Before you undergo the procedure, it's a good idea to learn more about it. Here's everything you need to know.

## 1. What is Botox?

Botox is basically an injection of botulinum toxin. It sounds scary, but it's safe for use as a cosmetic procedure and won't make you sick as it could if you ingest it from contaminated foods. It's commonly used to blur the signs of aging on your face and is one of the top anti-aging procedures chosen by both men and women in the United States and Canada.

## 2. How Will Botox Make Me Look Yonger?

![eyebrow, face, skin, cheek, forehead,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/pp/jm/mvgomy2l5b0dec3c15c8c479282262_600x390.jpg)

Botox is most often used on the face and is ideal for erasing crow's feet, frown lines and lines on your forehead. The treatment is designed to relax the areas where aging occurs without affecting the areas around it. Other reasons that Botox might be used include raising your eyebrow height, to counteract a downturned lip, to erase vertical lip lines. Be sure you talk to your Botox specialist about what you want to achieve with the treatment so you both understand the goal.

In some cases, additional treatments are required, such as fillers (Juvederm, Juvederm Plus, or Radiesse), or chemical peels to give you that youthful appearance you are seeking.

## 3. How Exactly Does It Slow down Aging?

The main goal of Botox is to reduce the appearance of lines and wrinkles on your face and that is done by halting the contraction of the muscles that form those lines. Not only does this hide the wrinkles you might already have, but it also keeps them from getting worse. This is because each time you contract the muscles that cause fine lines and wrinkles, you make them worse. By stopping those contractions from occurring, you can slow down the signs of aging while also hiding them from view. _Click here_ for more information.

## 4. What Will My Experience Be like?

You'll have to see a specialist to have Botox done and should be wary of any dermatologist offices or spas that offer it. You want to be sure your technician is certified and experienced. A very tiny needle is used to administer the Botox just under your skin and into your muscles. The needle causes minor muscle spasms as it halts the contraction of those muscles. The effects are usually visible a day or two after having Botox done. And it's just light discomfort that you are going to experience during the procedure.

## 5. How Long Does It Last?

_ Botox doesn't last forever so you'll have to get it done on a consistent basis for the best results at wrinkle removal_. Most people are able to go three to four months between treatments. You can set up an appropriate schedule for your needs with your technician. You should be able to see and feel when your current round of Botox is wearing off so that you can make an appointment to have it administered again.

## 6. Risks and Side Effects of Botox

Just as with any cosmetic procedure, Botox does carry a set of risks and side effects that you should be aware of before you opt to have it done. Side effects are generally minor and typically only occur when too much Botox is used or the needles are improperly placed. This includes swelling, bruising, pain and spreading of the Botox to unintended places. Other side effects include headaches, dry mouth and general malaise. Complications are also rare but include infection, weakness in nearby muscles, a droopy eyelid or lip, asymmetry or sensitivity.

## 7. Who Shouldn't Have Botox Done?

![eyebrow, face, cheek, nose, skin,](https://resize.allw.mn/1028x0/filters:format(webp)/filters:quality(70)/content/i4/ja/kk33pdyo5b0decfecb4c6706049258_600x461.jpg)

While Botox is deemed generally safe, there are certain people who shouldn't have it done. Pregnant or nursing mothers should wait until after they have delivered their baby and stopped breastfeeding before undergoing Botox. If you have health problems or skin conditions, talk about Botox with your doctor before choosing it.
