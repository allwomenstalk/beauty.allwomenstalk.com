# The easiest way to begin prepping your bikini area before the beach

You've found the perfect bathing suit, but you want to look your best in it. That's why you need to make sure that your bikini area is properly cared for. If it's not, then you could end up a bit embarrassed when you're sitting poolside worrying about pubic hair at the beach. If you've ever wondered _what is your bikini area_ exactly or _where is your bikini area_, it's generally the hair that extends beyond the panty line, but everyone defines it differently for their own comfort. Whether your goal is managing bathing suit pubes or achieving your ultimate bikini body goals, knowing how to prep your own bikini line is essential.

If you're unsure about how to tame your hair down there, here are a few different ways to prep your bikini area before beach season starts. You might want to keep these things in mind:

- Your comfort comes first, always.

- Preparation is just as important as the removal method.

- Aftercare is essential for smooth skin.

## 1. Trim

It doesn't matter if you're going to shave or wax, because the first thing you need to do is trim your hair. There are small grooming scissors that you can buy, so that you don't end up cutting yourself. You want to get your hair down to about 1/4 of an inch, so that it's easier for you to remove it when you get to the next step.

## 2. Shave

If you choose to shave, make sure that you take a shower first in order to soften the hair. You never ever want to shave dry, unless you don't mind bumps and cuts. Many women ask, "_can you shave before going to the beach?_" The answer is yes, but try to do it the night before to let your skin settle. Once your skin is soft enough, apply enough shaving gel to cover the area and make slow strokes along the direction of hair growth. You're going to have to bend and lift your legs in order to get certain areas, so try your best to be flexible. If you struggle with irritation, learning how to [prevent and treat ingrown hairs](https://beauty.allwomenstalk.com/how-to-prevent-and-treat-ingrown-hairs/) is a game changer.

## 3. Wax

If you don't want to shave, you can get a wax instead. Whether you're going for a simple cleanup or a full teeny bikini wax, it removes hair from the root. While it's much easier to go to the salon and have a professional do it, it can be embarrassing and expensive, which is why you can buy a kit to use at home. When you do, just make sure to test out a small area first to make sure you're not allergic to the product. If you're nervous, [getting your hair down to the right length](#1) beforehand can make the process easier.

## 4. Laser Hair Removal

If you have the money for laser hair removal, by all means, go for it. People often wonder _how do bikini models get rid of hair_, and professional laser treatments are frequently the answer. Just make sure that you do your research, because you don't want to go to a place that isn't rated highly. Places like [Ideal Image](https://www.idealimage.com/) offer consultations, but always read local reviews. If you're going to go for any type of surgery or permanent removal, don't skimp and only settle for the best.

## 5. Hair Removal Cream

There are hair removal creams that you can rub on your skin, leave for a few minutes, and then rub off. Brands like [Nair](https://www.naircare.com/) offer specific formulas for sensitive areas. While they usually don't have the best scents, this method might work for you if you're terrified of pain. Of course, just like you'd do when you're waxing, make sure to only test out a small spot at first to see if you're allergic to the product.

| Method | Pain Level | Lasting Time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Shaving | Low | 1-3 Days |
| Waxing | High | 3-6 Weeks |
| Cream | Low | 3-7 Days |

## 6. Alternatives

If you're not comfortable getting rid of your hair down there, there are plenty of bathing suits that have skirts attached. If you wear one of those, you won't have to worry about anyone seeing your bikini line. Not everyone has to wear teeny bikinis, you know. Finding [flattering swimsuits for your body type](https://beauty.allwomenstalk.com/best-swimsuits-for-your-body-type/) can make you feel just as confident, with or without hair removal.

## 7. Lotion

If you choose to remove your hair, make sure that you apply an unscented, moisturizing lotion on it afterward. There are also razor bump creams that you can buy in order to get rid of irritation and bumps on your skin.

Your skin is sensitive down there, so make sure that you aren't too harsh with yourself. Of course, you shouldn't be scared either, because it won't take long to get used to removing your hair. Do you have any tips for getting rid of hair from the bikini part of body?
