Frequently Asked Questions

What are couples tattoos?
Couples tattoos are matching or complementary tattoo designs that two people get to symbolize their relationship or love for each other.
Do couples tattoos have to match exactly?
No, couples tattoos don't have to match exactly. They can be matching, complementary, or even different designs that have a special meaning to the couple.
How do we choose a design for our couples tattoos?
You can pick something that means something special to both of you, like a date, a symbol of your love, or something you both enjoy. It's important to talk about it and agree on a design you'll both be happy with.
Are couple tattoos a good idea?
Couples tattoos can be a beautiful expression of love, but it's a big decision. Make sure you both really want the tattoo and think about how you'll feel about it in the future.
Can couples tattoos be a good way to show commitment?
Yes, getting a tattoo with your significant other can be a way to show commitment to the relationship, as tattoos are permanent and getting one is a serious decision.
What if my partner and I break up after getting a couples tattoo?
If that happens, some people choose to keep the tattoo as a memory, others get it covered up with a new design, or some go for removal. It's a personal choice.
How much do couples tattoos cost?
The cost can vary a lot based on the size, complexity of the design, and the shop you go to. It's best to discuss your design with a tattoo artist and get a price estimate.
Where should we get our couples tattoos?
You can get them anywhere you want, but think about how often you want to see them and show them off. Popular spots are the arm, wrist, chest, or anywhere else that feels meaningful to you.
How can we make sure our couples tattoos look good for a long time?
Take care of your tattoos by keeping them clean, moisturized, and protected from the sun. Listen to the aftercare advice your tattoo artist gives you.
Can we get couples tattoos if it's one person's first tattoo?
Yes, you can, but make sure the person getting their first tattoo is well-informed, comfortable with the process, and fully on board with the decision.
