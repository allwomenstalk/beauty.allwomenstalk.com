# 7 Expert Tips on How to Make Your Eyes Look Bigger and More Open

Ever wondered how some people always look so wide-awake and refreshed? The secret often lies in how they style their eyes. Whether you're dealing with naturally hooded lids or just want to enhance your natural beauty, learning **how to make your eyes look bigger** and brighter can transform your entire face. Mastering [natural makeup looks](https://beauty.allwomenstalk.com/natural-makeup-looks-for-beginners/) is a great first step in this journey.

Key tools to help your eyes look more open:

- High-quality eyelash curler

- Volumizing mascara

- White or nude eyeliner pencil

- Shimmering highlighter

## 1. Curl Your Eyelashes

Curling your eyelashes can really open up your eyes and is one of the fastest ways to see a difference. When it comes to **how to make your eyes bigger**, start by using a professional-grade [eyelash curler](https://www.tweezerman.com/classic-lash-curler.html) to shape your lashes. Aim for a natural curl that lifts from the root. Afterwards, apply a coat of mascara, as this will help create longer-looking lashes as well as opening up your eyes even further. Check out these [mascara tips for longer lashes](https://beauty.allwomenstalk.com/mascara-tips-for-longer-lashes/) to maximize the effect.

## 2. Dab on White Shadow

This tip is a favorite for makeup artists because it works instantly. Dab some white or light eye shadow or a [white eyeliner pencil](https://www.nyxcosmetics.com/eye/eyeliner/jumbo-eye-pencil/NYX_001.html) onto the inner corners of your eyes. This little trick makes eyes appear brighter and whiter, helping you achieve that coveted doe-eyed look.

## 3. Keep It Light and Bright

Keep the area around your eyes light and bright. Dark and heavy eye shadow can give the appearance of smaller eyes. If you want your eyes to appear bigger, maybe give that smoky eye shadow look a miss and stick to neutral or brighter hues. You can still work a bold makeup look with a bright shadow that complements your eye color. If you still want to create a natural smoky effect, try faintly smudging some kohl eyeliner on your lids instead of a heavy shadow.

| Technique | Effect | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Curling Lashes | Opens up the eye area | All eye shapes |
| White Shadow | Makes eyes look brighter | Inner corners |
| Highlighting | Accentuates brow bone | Creating lift |

## 4. Use Highlighters

Take advantage of all the different highlighters and illuminators on the market to help accentuate and **how to make eyes look more open**. Dab highlighter onto the inner corners of your eyes, along your brow bone, or even on the top of your cheek bones and blend in. The light-reflective properties of highlighters can help create the appearance of bigger, more open eyes.

## 5. Add Some Eyeliner

Enhance your eyes by adding a dark liner on your lids. Dark colors act as a contrast to the whites of your eyes and your eye color, making them more noticeable and bigger. Stick to pencil liners or a precise [liquid eyeliner](https://www.stilacosmetics.com/products/stay-all-day-waterproof-liquid-eye-liner) as they help create a more defined effect. Instead of circling your eyes completely, apply liner to your top lash line and add thickness as you move away from your nose to help **how to make eyes look longer** and more winged.

## 6. Try False Eyelashes

The reason so many people strive for longer lashes is the dramatic effect they have on our face. Longer lashes tend to give the effect of bigger eyes. Instead of heavy lash strips that can look a bit overwhelming, try adding a few individual lashes to the outer corners of your eyes to open them up for a more natural finish.

## 7. Groom Your Brows

Well-groomed eyebrows can really open up your face and provide a frame that makes everything look more balanced. If you’ve never had your eyebrows shaped before, consider visiting a professional. Once they’ve been properly shaped, follow this [eyebrow shaping guide](https://beauty.allwomenstalk.com/eyebrow-shaping-guide/) to maintain them at home. Create a natural arch by brushing them up and out, as this will help open up your face.

While we’re not all born as doe-eyed beauties, these are just some tips to help us create bigger and brighter looking eyes. Try out different techniques and see what works best for your unique eye shape. Do you have any favorite tricks for **how to make your eyes look bigger**? Share your tips in the comments!
