Frequently Asked Questions

What is a henna tattoo?
A henna tattoo is a kind of body art that uses a natural plant-based dye called henna. It's painted onto the skin in beautiful patterns and takes a few hours to dry. The tattoo is temporary and fades away after one to three weeks.
Is getting a henna tattoo safe?
Yes, getting a henna tattoo is usually safe if natural henna is used. However, some people might be allergic to henna. Black henna can contain harmful chemicals, so it's best to avoid it. Always do a patch test before applying it to a large area.
How long does a henna tattoo last?
A henna tattoo typically lasts between one to three weeks. How long it stays depends on your skin type, the quality of the henna, and how well you care for it after application.
Can henna tattoos be removed?
While henna tattoos fade over time, there isn't a quick way to remove them. You can help speed up the fading process by washing the area with soap and water, exfoliating gently, and avoiding chlorine and other harsh chemicals.
Are henna tattoos only for hands and feet?
No, henna tattoos can be applied to any part of the body. Hands and feet are traditional, but many people choose to have henna designs on their arms, back, or other areas.
